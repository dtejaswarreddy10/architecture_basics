Introduction

What is an NPU?

An NPU (Neural Processing Unit) is a processor specialized for AI math — the operations inside neural networks: matrix multiplications, convolutions, and activations. A CPU is a general thinker and a GPU is a parallel cruncher; an NPU is a dedicated AI engine that does one family of math extremely fast and efficiently.

Where did NPUs come from?

NPUs appeared because AI moved onto everyday devices — phones needed face unlock, photo enhancement, and voice assistants instantly without draining the battery.

CPU-only AI

Too slow for real-time on-device inference.

GPU on mobile

Fast, but used too much power for always-on AI.

Dedicated NPU block

A small, efficient AI engine added straight into the SoC.

AI everywhere

On-device, private AI at tiny power budgets.

The main idea behind NPU design

Do neural-network math with maximum performance-per-watt — hard-wire the exact operations AI needs (massive multiply-accumulate) and use low-precision numbers to save power and area.

Basic Functionality & Architecture

Simple analogy: a dedicated AI assembly line

A GPU is a general factory. An NPU is a purpose-built assembly line that makes only one product — neural-network results — so every part is tuned for that single job.

The core building block: MAC arrays

Neural networks are mostly multiply-and-accumulate (MAC) operations — output = Σ (wᵢ × xᵢ). An NPU packs huge arrays of MAC units plus dedicated convolution and activation hardware.

Applications

On-device / edge AI — the sweet spot

NPUs shine where AI must run locally, instantly, and at low power.

📷 Computational photography 🗣️ Voice assistants 🔐 Face unlock & biometrics 🌐 Live translation 🤖 On-device generative AI

✅ Why on-device AI wins

  • Privacy — data never leaves the device
  • Latency — no cloud round-trip
  • Battery — far more efficient
  • Offline — works with no network

⛔ Not good at

  • General-purpose programs → CPU
  • Graphics / broad compute → GPU
  • Large-scale training → GPU/TPU
  • Non-neural-shaped math

Summary

NPU in one paragraph

An NPU is a processor purpose-built for neural-network math. It packs large arrays of multiply-accumulate units and low-precision arithmetic to run AI inference with the best possible performance-per-watt. By specializing, it gives up general flexibility but enables fast, private, battery-friendly AI directly on phones, PCs, and edge devices.

Choose an NPU when…

  • You run AI inference on-device
  • Power & battery matter
  • You need low latency & privacy

Future trends

  • On-device generative AI as normal
  • Hundreds of TOPS in consumer chips
  • Ultra-low precision (INT4, FP8)
  • Standard SoC block beside CPU + GPU
