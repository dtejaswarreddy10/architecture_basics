1 What is an NPU?

An NPU is a processor specialized for AI math — the operations inside neural networks: matrix multiplications, convolutions, and activations.

A CPU is a general thinker and a GPU is a parallel cruncher — an NPU is a dedicated AI engine that does one family of math extremely fast and efficiently.

The main idea

Do neural-network math with maximum performance-per-watt — hard-wire the exact operations AI needs (massive multiply-accumulate) and use low-precision numbers to save power and area.

3 Inside an NPU: MAC arrays

Neural networks are mostly multiply-and-accumulate (MAC) operations — output = Σ (wᵢ × xᵢ). An NPU packs huge arrays of MAC units plus dedicated convolution and activation hardware.

Inputs stream through layers of multiply-accumulate units → activations → outputs — every stage tuned for one job.

4 Low precision = efficiency

Smaller numbers mean less power and chip area. NPUs lean on low-precision arithmetic:

6 Applications

NPUs shine where AI must run locally, instantly, and at low power:

📷 Computational photography 🗣️ Voice assistants 🔐 Face unlock & biometrics 🌐 Live translation 🤖 On-device generative AI

✅ Why on-device AI wins

  • Privacy — data never leaves the device
  • Latency — no cloud round-trip
  • Battery — far more efficient
  • Offline — works with no network

⛔ Not ideal for

  • General-purpose programs → CPU
  • Graphics / broad compute → GPU
  • Large-scale training → GPU/TPU
  • Non-neural-shaped math

8 NPU in one paragraph

An NPU is a processor purpose-built for neural-network math. It packs large arrays of multiply-accumulate units and low-precision arithmetic to run AI inference with the best possible performance-per-watt — trading general flexibility for fast, private, battery-friendly AI on phones, PCs, and edge devices.

Choose an NPU for…

  • AI inference on-device
  • Power- & battery-bound apps
  • Low latency & privacy
  • Offline, no-network use

Future trends

  • On-device generative AI as normal
  • Hundreds of TOPS in consumer chips
  • Ultra-low precision (INT4, FP8)
  • Standard SoC block with CPU + GPU
