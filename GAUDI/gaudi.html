Introduction

What is Gaudi?

Gaudi is a family of AI training & inference accelerators from Habana Labs, a company acquired by Intel in 2019. It competes with GPUs for deep learning, but its standout feature is huge amounts of integrated RoCE Ethernet built directly into the processor for scaling out.

Where did it come from?

Habana Labs (Israel, 2016) set out to build cost-effective, easy-to-scale deep-learning silicon. Intel acquired Habana in 2019 and made Gaudi its flagship data-center training accelerator, emphasizing price/performance versus NVIDIA.

The main idea

Make scaling cheap and open by putting networking on the chip. Gaudi pairs specialized matrix & vector engines with a large amount of on-die Ethernet, so clusters use standard switches instead of proprietary fabrics.

Summary

Gaudi in one paragraph

Gaudi is Intel/Habana's deep-learning accelerator that pairs a Matrix Math Engine and programmable Tensor Processor Cores with HBM — and crucially integrates large amounts of standard RoCE Ethernet directly on-chip (Gaudi2: 24× 100GbE). That on-die networking lets data centers scale across many accelerators using ordinary Ethernet switches, giving Gaudi a price/performance and openness edge over GPUs that rely on proprietary interconnects.

Choose Gaudi when…

  • You scale across many accelerators
  • You want standard Ethernet scaling
  • Price/performance matters
  • You build open AI infrastructure

Future trends

  • Open, Ethernet-based AI networking
  • Price/performance competition
  • Bigger memory & compute per generation
  • Converging AI accelerator roadmaps
