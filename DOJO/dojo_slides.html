1 What is Dojo?

Dojo is a custom AI training supercomputer built by Tesla to train the vision networks behind Full Self-Driving (FSD) and Autopilot.

It isn't a product you can buy — it was an internal machine built from Tesla's own D1 chip, optimized for one job: training on video at massive scale.

Build a seamless, tileable fabric of compute optimized for one workload — removing the boundaries between chips so many dies behave like one huge surface.

2 Where Dojo came from

Tesla collects vast camera video from its fleet. Training on that data demanded enormous compute, so rather than only buying GPUs, Tesla designed a vertically integrated system — its own chip, board, and interconnect — tuned for video-heavy training.

Analogy: building with LEGO tiles

Dojo scales like snapping LEGO tiles together: one block (a D1 chip) repeats into a tile, tiles snap into cabinets, cabinets combine into a full system — each level a bigger, seamless version of the same thing.

⚠️ Status (2026): Tesla reportedly wound down its dedicated Dojo program in 2025 — yet it remains an influential example of vertically integrated AI-training hardware.

8 Dojo in one paragraph

Dojo was Tesla's custom AI training supercomputer, built from its own D1 chip and scaled by tiling — 25 D1 dies form a seamless Training Tile, tiles fill cabinets, and many cabinets make an ExaPOD. Connected by a high-bandwidth mesh and using a configurable CFP8 datatype, it was tuned for training Tesla's video-based self-driving networks — a bold example of vertical integration, though Tesla reportedly wound the dedicated program down in 2025.

A bespoke machine fits when…

  • You have a single dominant workload
  • You own enormous proprietary data
  • You want full-stack control
  • You can absorb the cost & risk

Future trends

  • Tiling / mesh scaling lives on
  • Custom training datatypes spreading
  • More exploring custom silicon — cautiously
  • Build-vs-buy stays a key question
