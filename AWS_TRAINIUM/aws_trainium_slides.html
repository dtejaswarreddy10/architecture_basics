1 What are Trainium & Inferentia?

They are custom AI ASICs designed by Amazon (AWS): Inferentia is optimized for inference (running trained models cheaply), and Trainium for training (building & fine-tuning models).

You don't buy them — you rent them as AWS instances.

The main idea

Split the AI lifecycle into two specialized chips and run them only in AWS — tailored to AWS data centers for better price/performance than general-purpose GPUs.

2 Where they came from

After acquiring Annapurna Labs (2015), Amazon built a custom-silicon program — also behind Nitro and Graviton.

As GPUs grew scarce and costly, AWS built its own AI chips to control cost, supply, and efficiency.

Analogy: a gym vs a stadium

Training (Trainium) is the gym — heavy work to build the model. Inference (Inferentia) is the stadium — the trained model now performs, over and over. Two jobs → two chips.

3 Two chips, one cloud

The AI lifecycle is split across two complementary chips, both running only inside AWS:

🏋️ Trainium

  • Job: train & fine-tune
  • Instances: Trn1 / Trn2
  • Scales into UltraClusters

⚡ Inferentia

  • Job: run trained models
  • Instances: Inf1 / Inf2
  • High-throughput serving
Both built on NeuronCores, linked by NeuronLink, and programmed via the AWS Neuron SDK (PyTorch / JAX).

8 In one paragraph

Trainium and Inferentia are Amazon's custom AI ASICs, available only on AWS: Inferentia is tuned for cheap, high-throughput inference, while Trainium targets large-scale training. Built around NeuronCores, linked by NeuronLink into UltraClusters, and programmed through the AWS Neuron SDK, they give a lower-cost, supply-controlled alternative to GPUs for both phases of the AI lifecycle.

Choose them when…

  • You run AI on AWS
  • You want lower cost than GPU instances
  • You need training or inference
  • You scale to thousands of chips

Future trends

  • More hyperscaler custom AI chips
  • Larger training UltraClusters
  • Better price/performance each gen
  • Deeper framework integration
