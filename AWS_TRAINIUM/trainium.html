Trainium & Inferentia

Amazon's own AI chips, available only in AWS — two complementary processors: Trainium for training, Inferentia for inference — built to cut the cost of cloud AI versus renting GPUs.

🏋️ Trainium = training ⚡ Inferentia = inference ☁️ AWS-only 💰 Lower cost vs GPUs
1

Introduction

What are Trainium & Inferentia?

They are custom AI ASICs designed by Amazon (AWS): Inferentia is optimized for inference (running trained models cheaply), and Trainium is optimized for training (building & fine-tuning models). You don't buy them — you rent them as AWS instances.

Where did they come from?

After acquiring Annapurna Labs (2015), Amazon built a custom-silicon program (also behind Nitro and Graviton). As GPUs grew scarce and costly, AWS built its own AI chips to control cost, supply, and efficiency.

The main idea

Split the AI lifecycle into two specialized chips and run them only in AWS. By owning the silicon, AWS tailors the chips to its data centers and offers better price/performance than general-purpose GPUs for many workloads.
2

Basic Functionality & Architecture

Simple analogy: a gym vs a stadium

Training (Trainium) is the gym — intense, heavy work to build the athlete (the model). Inference (Inferentia) is the stadium — the trained athlete now performs, over and over, efficiently. Two jobs → two chips.

Applications

🏋️ Trainium — training & fine-tuning LLMs ⚡ Inferentia — chatbots, search, vision ☁️ AWS-native AI at scale 💰 Cost-sensitive deployments 🤝 Frontier model training

✅ Why pick them

  • Cheaper than GPUs for many AWS workloads
  • Massive scale via UltraClusters
  • Managed — integrated into AWS
  • Two chips tuned to the two phases of AI

⛔ Not for

  • Use outside AWS — cloud-only
  • General computing → CPU / Graviton
  • Graphics → GPU
  • Widest framework ecosystem (CUDA)

Summary

Trainium & Inferentia in one paragraph

Trainium and Inferentia are Amazon's custom AI ASICs, available only on AWS: Inferentia is tuned for cheap, high-throughput inference, while Trainium targets large-scale training. Built around NeuronCores, linked by NeuronLink into UltraClusters, and programmed through the AWS Neuron SDK, they give cloud customers a lower-cost, supply-controlled alternative to GPUs for both phases of the AI lifecycle.

Choose them when…

  • You run AI on AWS
  • You want lower cost than GPU instances
  • You need training or inference specifically
  • You scale to thousands of chips

Future trends

  • More hyperscaler custom AI chips
  • Larger training UltraClusters
  • Better price/performance each generation
  • Deeper framework integration
