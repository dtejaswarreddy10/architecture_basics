1 What is a TPU?

A TPU (Tensor Processing Unit) is a custom-built ASIC designed by Google to accelerate machine-learning workloads — especially the tensor (matrix) operations at the heart of neural networks.

A GPU is a general parallel processor adapted for AI; a TPU is built from scratch for AI, nothing else.

The main idea

Specialize completely around the matrix multiply — pour the silicon into one giant systolic array that multiplies matrices with minimal data movement, the most expensive part of running a neural network.

2 Where TPUs came from

Around 2013, Google realized that a few minutes of daily voice search per user would double their data-center compute needs.

CPUs and GPUs were too costly and power-hungry at that scale — so Google designed its own chip.

Powering Google ever since

The first TPU has driven Search, Translate, Photos — and now Gemini.

3 The systolic array

Like a bucket brigade passing water hand-to-hand: data flows step by step through a grid of small multiply units, so the chip rarely touches memory.

Loadweights stay in cells
Streaminputs flow in
Multiplyeach cell does a MAC
Accumulatepartial sums march

The Matrix Multiply Unit (MXU)

A large 2-D grid — e.g. 128 × 128 = 16,384 multipliers. Weights stay put; data flows through — keeping work on-chip and dodging the memory bottleneck.

6 Applications

What TPUs are built for:

🧠 Training large networks ⚡ Inference at Google scale 🔬 Cloud TPU & Colab research 🏢 Large batch AI
Scale is the point: TPUs link into "pods" — thousands of chips on fast interconnect, acting as one giant AI supercomputer.

✅ Great at

  • Matrix-heavy neural nets
  • Training & inference at scale
  • Efficiency per watt & per dollar
  • TensorFlow / JAX workloads

⛔ Not good at

  • General-purpose computing → CPU
  • Graphics / rendering → GPU
  • Branch-heavy, irregular code
  • Anything outside Google Cloud

8 TPU in one paragraph

A TPU is Google's custom AI accelerator (ASIC) built around a systolic array that performs matrix multiplication with minimal data movement. By specializing entirely on tensor math and low-precision formats like BF16, it trains and serves neural networks with exceptional efficiency at data-center scale — at the cost of being inflexible and available only through Google Cloud.

Choose a TPU when…

  • You train/serve large neural nets
  • You work in Google Cloud / JAX / TF
  • You want efficiency at scale (pods)

Future trends

  • Ever-larger pods as one supercomputer
  • Perf-per-watt leaps (Trillium → beyond)
  • Tight integration with Gemini
  • The shift to custom AI silicon
